SUMMARY
These are the prototypes of a desktop website and mobile app for a potential furniture store.
Their design is a project assignment, part of Daniel Scott’s online course “UX Design Essentials”, on Skillshare.

The Client

Maynooth Furniture is a new business selling affordable, high-end design furniture made in Ireland. Their primary objective is to build an e-commerce website and/or app for people to browse and purchase furniture for home delivery.

The Concept

Maynooth produces both craftsman and line furniture. Regardless of their production method, at the heart of their business is making furniture that’s properly finished, has a long life and will retain its value. They use top quality materials, like solid hardwoods and abiding techniques, like dovetail and tenon joints.

They care about their pieces and the places they will occupy in each of their customers homes, that’s why they want to provide as much information as possible about their physical characteristics and care instructions, to help the customer envision the “fleshed-out” piece directly in their home, make an informed decision about their purchase and have the know-how to preserve it in good shape for a long time.

As an extension of their interest in the piece’s well-being, they take the responsibility to provide the transportation, under a standard time table, but the customers can also choose a custom delivery time, at extra cost.

The Competition

The competition in the furniture business is strong, with many local names and international ones as well, like: www.made.com, www.habitat.eu.

Maynooth wants to place against its competitors with custom deliveries, thorough - but easily accessible - product descriptions and inspiration ideas that will help the customers make judicious decisions about their acquisitions.

Target Audience

The store focuses on middle class people who appreciate durability, practicality and good taste.

These are autonomous individuals, who enjoy a comfortable (but not extravagant) life-style, they usually own a house and they are both style and price conscientious and are careful to make educated purchases.

They value proffessionalism, effort and responsibility and become loyal customers to brands that fit this description.

Organising the information

The first step in creating a structure for the new website was identifying the correct functional and content requirements.

Being an e-commerce website, some of these requirements are implicit, like proper product categorisation, relevant product descriptions (with prices and pictures) and highlight deals and clearances.

But the specificities of the furniture store also brings new requirements, like: proper classification of the broad product information, possibility to schedule the delivery, provide inspiration ideas and offer means of saving and revisit the design ideas.

SKETCHES

I started the website’s layout by drawing the sketches of three of its pages: the home page, one of the category pages (the “Bedroom”) and a product page.

WIREFRAMES

I used Adobe XD to create both the wireframes and the final mockup, afterwards.

There are slight changes in the wireframes, compared to the sketches, the most significant one being the reviews section of the product page. This section I identified it necessary in the meantime, by interviewing few of my close relatives that fit the persona description.

The reviews section sustains the user need of gaining even more insight about the product, based on subjective opinions and experiences shared by similar people.

HOME PAGE

The home page is structured in several sections that are in line with client requests. First hand, it shows a hero slider where the current store offers are announced on a background displaying beautiful pictures of furniture items. Then continues with the newly introduced products available on store. Next section is a shortcut for the main product categories. Then comes the clearances, and lastly, the inspiration ideas.

Each of these sections have corresponding call-to-action buttons, offering guidance for visitors who are ready to purchase. The colours for each of the CTA buttons is not random, they were chosen based on the brand colours and they’re meant to support the sections they’re in. For example, the “new in store” section has the blue-green hues (#4CB3AA) representing “freshness”. The inspiration ideas has purple (#842C68), which evokes creativity and sophistication. The clearance and offers sections have fuchsia (#CC4C6E) and yellow (#F3B541), as they are both “alert” colours and induce a greater sense of urgency than the rest.

CATEGORY PAGE

The category page presents the user with corresponding products found in that category. The page was designed with multiple filtering and sorting options. The filter & sort section is visually separated on the left side by a discreet gradient, consistent with the area markers used in the home page. Further search customisation is available through a series of sub-categories, meant to bring an even greater level of granularity to the search result sets.

All of these refinements have the purpose of aiding the user in its search, help him to find faster a suitable product compatible with his needs.

PRODUCT PAGE

All the information about the product’s physical, qualitative and commercial characteristics is collected in this page. This information is available both through real-life images of the item and descriptive text.

The pictures can be zoomed-in, added to favourites or pinned directly in the user’s Pinterest account, for later revisits. The product’s description comprises also a considerable amount of written details, as a succession of facets grouped in relevant categories (like dimensions, care instructions etc.).

The page continues with a section for similar products viewed by other users and ends with the product reviews section, where past buyers offer their subjective opinions about the selected product. To further support the need for relevant and compact information, this section allows a search through the review comments and offers a few search suggestions on the most significant topics.

All of this is meant to inform the buyer in the best comprehensive way possible, before committing to a purchase.

The Progress

The prototypes of both the website and the mobile app were completed in roughly 6 weeks.
This version of the product includes the feedback from other course participants and from the professor himself. The first feedback was received for my wireframes, on the Skillshare platform. I got a helpful hint about the hero image section and a very encouraging thumbs up from the course creator and teacher:

Second feedback was received for an older versions of the website, that did not have the hero section groomed yet to look attractive.

The constructive feedback was focused solely on the hero part, which led me to later reworks to upscale it into the version that exists now.

On the third and final feedback round, the mobile app prototype received positive reviews, some of the most applauded parts mentioning its navigation, comprehensiveness and attention to details.

Conclusion

Working on these prototypes, I learned more about how important the first impression is on a commercial website and for that matter, the hero section requires a greater design investment than I initially anticipated.
